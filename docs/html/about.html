Research Assistant, Dr. Jamie Near

April 2019 – April 2020

My most recent research experience was with Dr. Near at the Douglas Mental Heatlh University Institute. I worked on extending functionallity of FID-A, a MRS toolbox, to process MRSI data as well. MRSI is a relativly novel technique that obtains a brain image of spacial motabolite concentrations. The toolbox creates a processing pipeline for MRSI data that allows researchers to fully process and visualizer their scans. An addiional function was a creation of a simulation package that can take any MRSI scan and calculuates scan metrics, like scan time and magnetic gradient strength.

Honours Project, Dr. Jerome Waldispuhl

September 2019 - April 2020

For my honours project, I was given the opprotunity to work on a game called Ribo. Ribo leverages human pattern recognition to help solve the problem of aligning multiple sequences of DNA. It sends sections of DNA to the player (5-10 sequneces, each around 50-100 nucleotides long) to align. In Unity, I built tools for players than can help them increase their alignment scores and complete puzzles faster. Further, I implemented a new schoring scheme to Ribo that insentivies better alignments then the last.

Research Intern, Dr. Sébastien Lemieux

June 2018 - August 2018

At the Institute for Research in Immunology and Cancer, I worked on testing a novel algorithm called km. I used samples for The Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA) dataset to test km on common known mutations which were found on the Genomic Commons Data Portal. I would test this algorithm using cluster computing and report on the accuracy and specificity. Further, I developed a new tool for the algorithm that detected gene fusion mutations. I utilized a breadth first search to determine if there were connections between two exons in RNA-Seq data.
