Multi-Contrast MRI Reconstruction Using Self-Supervised Learning and Attention Networks

Smith J., Kadota B., Johnson R., Williams T.

Journal of Magnetic Resonance Imaging, Vol. 41, Issue 3

This paper introduces a novel approach for multi-contrast MRI reconstruction using deep learning. By leveraging the inherent relationships between different MRI contrasts, our method achieves up to 8x acceleration while maintaining diagnostic quality. Our attention-based neural network architecture effectively captures spatial and contrast dependencies, outperforming current state-of-the-art methods on both quantitative metrics and radiologist evaluations.

2022

Compressed Memory Networks for Accelerated Cardiac MRI Video Reconstruction

Kadota B., Chen A., Thompson P.

IEEE Transactions on Medical Imaging, Vol. 37, Issue 8

Cardiac MRI videos provide crucial information about heart function but require lengthy acquisition times. In this work, we develop a compressed memory network that leverages the temporal redundancy in cardiac videos to achieve 4x acceleration while reducing memory requirements by 3x compared to conventional approaches. Our method demonstrates superior performance in preserving dynamic cardiac features critical for clinical assessment.

2021

Self-Supervised Pretraining for Improved MRI Reconstruction from Undersampled Data

Garcia M., Kadota B., Lee S., Brown D.

Medical Image Computing and Computer Assisted Intervention (MICCAI), pp. 418-427

We propose a self-supervised pretraining approach that significantly improves deep learning-based MRI reconstruction from undersampled k-space data. By learning anatomical priors from large unlabeled MRI datasets, our method reduces the need for fully-sampled ground truth data during training. Results show improved reconstruction quality across multiple anatomies and acceleration factors compared to supervised approaches trained on limited data.
